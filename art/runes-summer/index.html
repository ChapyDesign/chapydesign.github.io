Runes Summer

A quiet postcard from the 2024 crypto winter.

Runes Summer is a looping, browser-based digital artwork built entirely in p5.js that captures the strange calm that fell over the Ordinals and Runes ecosystem once the frenzy died down. A tattooed woman in red-and-blue 3D glasses, the unofficial mascot of every NFT bull run,stands knee-deep in bright water, grinning for one last tourist photo next to a towering, bird-poop-splattered Runestone. The stone, once the most talked-about airdrop on Bitcoin Twitter and the key to the free DOG rune, now just sits there while two gulls drift back and forth overhead.

Everything you see is driven by code. I started with a 1950s bikini photo that I completely redrew and re-imagined – adding classic sailor tattoos, the anachronistic 3D glasses, and a subtle halftone newspaper texture so she feels like a memory lifted from an old magazine. The Runestone and birds are masked photographs, placed by hand. From there the scene comes to life: multiple layers of sine-wave ripples (with different frequencies and slight Perlin noise offsets) radiate from her legs and the base of the stone, the birds glide on smooth back-and-forth paths with gentle randomness, and an interactive splash of blue particles appears wherever you click in the water.

Small, self-contained, and running at 60 fps in any browser, it’s equal parts nostalgia, gentle satire, and a quiet love letter to making art one line of code at a time.
